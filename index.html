Help

Controls

Computer

  • Left click and drag circle: change position of circle on the pitch
  • Double left click on circle: change number in circle
  • Right click and drag from circle: Draw dotted arrow from circle
  • Right click on circle: Remove oldest arrow from circle (if two arrows are drawn)
  • Double right click on circle: Remove arrows from circle

    • Mobile

    Use the following buttons to switch between dragging the circle and drawing an arrow:

  • Drag a circle
  • Draw an arrow

    • Dragging circle

  • Hold and drag circle: change position of circle on the pitch
  • Double tap on circle: change number in circle

    • Drawing arrow

  • Hold and drag: Draw arrow from the circle
  • Single tap on circle: Remove oldest arrow from the circle
  • Double tap on circle: Remove all arrows from the circle

    • Importing team

    You can directly import a team through a saved .JSON file.

    JSON files need to be imported in one of the two following formats:

    
          
    
          
    
            
    
          
    
        
    
        
    IMPORTANT NOTE: Make sure the labels (or keys) are as shown in the template
          (i.e. #1, #2, etc. to #11). The
          tool will not recognize the JSON if they are changed.
    
        
    Be aware that each formation has a predetermined position for each number (for example, the #11 will
          always be
          the left striker in a 4-4-2 and a left winger in a 4-3-3). If you download a team, this will be
          automatically generated for you. The order of the labels and their corresponding players in the .JSON is
          irrelevant. The
          #7 in the JSON will always match the #7 in the lineup, whether it's the first or last in the JSON.
    
        
    Details
    
        
    teamName: Put your team name here
    
        
    formation:  Recognized inputs for formations are:
    
        
  • 433
    • 
        
  • 442flat
    • 
        
  • 442diamond
    • 
        
  • 451
    • 
        
  • 4231
    • 
        
  • 532
    • 
        
  • 343
    • 
        
  • 3421
    • 
        
    Dashes and spaces will be ignored (so for example, '4-4-2 diamond' will also work). Other formations
          will
          be defaulted to a 4-3-3.
    
        
    starting: Put your starting players in the positions linked to that number.
    
        
    For the starters, you can also specify their shirt number (or any other label (for example: RW) you
          want to be shown in
          the circle)
    
        
    colors: 
    
        
  • mainColor: the filling of the circle
    • 
        
  • secondColor: the border of the circle
    • 
        
  • numberColor: the number/label within the circle
    • 
        
    Colors can be in words ['red', 'blue'], RGB [rgb(255, 0, 0)] (you can also use percentages) and
          hexadecimals [#ff0000].
    
        
    secondType: Either 'back-up' or 'opposition'. Other inputs will be defaulted to 'back-up'.
        
    second: Based on the input on secondType, this will contain either the back-up players or the
          opposition players.
          For back-up players, you can just add the name to the number as shown in the example.
          For opposition players you will have to specify the name and number similar to how it's done for the
          starting players.
    
        
    oppoFormation:  The formation that will be used for the opposition team. The recognized inputs
          are
          the same as specified above.
    
        
    oppoColors:  Uses the same format as colors for the main team.
    
        
    Optional:
    
        
    circlePositions:  When downloading your team, a string containing the specific position of the
          circles on the pitch is created. This can be used to load your circles in specific positions as opposed
          to predetermined positions from formations.
    
        
    oppoCirclePositions: The same as circlePositions, but for opposition circles.
    
        
    
        
    Download
    
        
    You can download your team in two filetypes: .JSON and .PNG. The .JSON will be downloaded in a similar
          format as above based on your input when downloading.
          This can be useful if you want to continue working on your team later.
    
        
    The .PNG will take a snapshot of the pitch on the right and can be used for sharing your team with
          others.
